Dexter Glossary

The most useful terms for the Dexter platform.
Automatic Tool Changer (ATC) A "Hand" that mounts on the end of Dexter's last link that can grab any end-effector that has a cooresponding ATC handle. This enables Dexter to easily grab many different end-effectors. Dexter's ATC can pass both electrical signals and power to an end-effector, and receive electrical signals from sensors in an end-effector.
arcsecond A unit of angle, meaning one 3600th of a degree. This is about the resolution of a Dexter joint. There are 1,296,000 arcseconds in 360 degrees.
Cartesian Coordinate System
config (for Dexter's joint angles) The position of Dexter's end effector does not fully describe its physical state. There can be several different ways in which Dexter's joints can be bent to arrive at an end position, and the config determines that. It is an array of 3 integers, all either 0 or 1. The first number means LEFT (0) or RIGHT (1). The second means DOWN (0) or UP (1). The third means IN (0) or OUT (1). See the Reference Manual for Dexter.move_to for more info.
do_list The property of a Job instance that holds the array of instructions to be executed by the Job. Usually the instructions are run in order, though various control instructions can skip over, conditionally run, or even loop through, do_list items. The do_list is the essence of a Job.
end-effector A tool held by Dexter for performing a specific function such as a gripper for holding things or a laser for cutting things. See ATC.
instruction Generally, an item on a Job's do_list describing a small action such as Dexter.move_to a location. Some do_list items are literally an instance of DDE's Instruction class, while others, like functions and arrays, are common JavaScript types.
Job The data structure that describes the process of making (or doing) something. Jobs have many properties, all of which have defaults. The most important ones are:
  • name
  • do_list
  • robot
J5_direction An array of 2 or 3 numbers that indicates the orientation of Dexter's joint 5 (or its last link). See the Reference Manual for Dexter.move_to for more info.
kinemetics The relationship between the joint angles and the position of a robot. Forward kinematics is the computation for tranforming joint angles into end effector location (in Cartesian coorinates). Inverse kinematics is the computation for tranforming Cartesian coordinates of an end effector into joint angles. These computations are complex, especially for Robots with 5 or more axes.
micron A unit of length, meaning one milionth of a meter.
pose A 4 x 4 matrix of numbers used to efficiently describe Dexter's physical state.
position A 3 x 3 matrix of numbers describing Dexter's state. The first row is the xyz location of the end effector. The second row is the J5_direction of the end effector. the thrid row is the config.
robot A machine that can execute instructions. The fundamental definition of a robot is its instruction set, i.e. the instructions that it is capable of executing. A Job feeds instructions from its do_list to its robot to run.
SI Units The International scientific units standard. Generally, Dexter uses these except for angles instead of the SI radians, we use degrees. DDE has a easy way to convert numbers between different units.
xyz A location expressed as a 3 element array of numbers in cartesian coordinates. Often xyz refers to the location of Dexter's end effector relative to the location of joint 1. If you are behind Dexter with its processor board to the right, positive X is to your right, positive Y is straight ahead, and postive Z is up.
